<p>The <b>plumed whistling duck</b> (<i><b>Dendrocygna eytoni</b></i>), also called the <b>grass whistling duck</b>, is a species of bird in the <a href="/wiki/Whistling_duck" title="Whistling duck">whistling duck</a> subfamily of the family <a href="/wiki/Anatidae" title="Anatidae">Anatidae</a>. It is resident and breeds in <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australia</a>, in a broad arc covering the northern half and most of the eastern third of the continent. There are also non-breeding and seasonally uncertain populations in <a href="/wiki/New_Zealand" title="New Zealand">New Zealand</a>, <a href="/wiki/Solomon_Islands" title="Solomon Islands">the Solomon Islands</a>, <a href="/wiki/Indonesia" title="Indonesia">Indonesia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Papua_New_Guinea" title="Papua New Guinea">Papua New Guinea</a>.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-iucn&#95;status&#95;12&#95;November&#95;2021&#95;1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-iucn_status_12_November_2021-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> It is a predominantly brown-coloured duck with a long neck and characteristic <a href="/wiki/Plumage" title="Plumage">plumes</a> arising from its flanks. The sexes are similar in appearance.
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Taxonomy">Taxonomy</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Plumed_whistling_duck&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Taxonomy"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>
<p>Described by English naturalist <a href="/wiki/Thomas_Campbell_Eyton" title="Thomas Campbell Eyton">Thomas Campbell Eyton</a> in 1838, its specific epithet honours its namer. Its generic name is derived from the <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Greek" title="Ancient Greek">Ancient Greek</a> terms <span title="Ancient Greek (to 1453)-language text"><i lang="grc-Latn">dendron</i></span> "tree", and <span title="Ancient Greek (to 1453)-language text"><i lang="grc-Latn">kuknos</i></span> (via <a href="/wiki/Latin" title="Latin">Latin</a> <span title="Latin-language text"><i lang="la">cygnus</i></span>) "swan".<sup id="cite&#95;ref-Liddell1980&#95;2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Liddell1980-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Alternate common names include; Eyton's plumed, red-legged or whistling tree-duck, and grey or red-legged whistler. 
</p>
<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Description">Description</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Plumed_whistling_duck&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Description"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>
<p>Measuring 42–60&#160;cm (16.5–23.5&#160;in) and weighing around one kilogram (2.2&#160;lb), it is a long-necked duck with brown upperparts, paler underparts and a white rump. The chest is a chestnut colour with thin black bars, while long black-margined plumes arise from its flanks. Its bill and legs are a pinkish red, and the <a href="/wiki/Iris_(anatomy)" title="Iris (anatomy)">iris</a> of the eyes are yellow. The male and female are similar in appearance.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-Simpsday&#95;3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Simpsday-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The species has a characteristic lowered neck and short, dark, rounded wings while flying.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-Slater70&#95;4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Slater70-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/Bird_vocalization" title="Bird vocalization">call</a> is a characteristic <a href="/wiki/Whistle" title="Whistle">whistle</a>, which gives the bird its common name.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-Slater70&#95;4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Slater70-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p>

<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Distribution_and_habitat">Distribution and habitat</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Plumed_whistling_duck&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Distribution and habitat"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>
<p>The plumed whistling duck has a range across the eastern, northern and central parts of Australia from the <a href="/wiki/Kimberley_(Western_Australia)" title="Kimberley (Western Australia)">Kimberley</a> across the <a href="/wiki/Top_End" title="Top End">Top End</a> and <a href="/wiki/Cape_York_Peninsula" title="Cape York Peninsula">Cape York</a>, down to southern <a href="/wiki/Queensland" title="Queensland">Queensland</a> and northern <a href="/wiki/New_South_Wales" title="New South Wales">New South Wales</a> on the east coast, although may reach north-western <a href="/wiki/Victoria_(Australia)" class="mw-redirect" title="Victoria (Australia)">Victoria</a> inland, in the vicinity of the <a href="/wiki/Murray_River" title="Murray River">Murray River</a>.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-Ber93&#95;5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Ber93-5"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> It is also found in New Guinea. The preferred habitat is tall <a href="/wiki/Grassland" title="Grassland">grassland</a> and <a href="/wiki/Savanna" title="Savanna">savanna</a>, often near bodies of water.
</p>
<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Feeding">Feeding</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Plumed_whistling_duck&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Feeding"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>
<p>Rather than <a href="/wiki/Diving_bird" title="Diving bird">diving</a> for food in bodies of water like other ducks, the plumed whistling duck feeds by cropping grass on land.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>6<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p>
<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Breeding">Breeding</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Plumed_whistling_duck&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Breeding"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>
<p>The plumed whistling duck breeds during the <a href="/wiki/Wet_season" title="Wet season">wet season</a>, generally in January to March, although it can be later in April or, in a few cases, May. One brood is raised per season. The nest is a mattress of grasses or similar material in tall grass, or in or near vegetation as cover. Ten to 12 oval <a href="/wiki/Egg_(biology)" class="mw-redirect" title="Egg (biology)">eggs</a> are laid, measuring 48 by 36 millimetres (1.9&#160;in ×&#160;1.4&#160;in); 14 or more have been recorded on occasion. Initially shiny and creamy-coloured, they may become stained.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-Ber93&#95;5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Ber93-5"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Avian_incubation" class="mw-redirect" title="Avian incubation">incubation</a> period is around 30 days.
</p>
